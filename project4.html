An Introduction to Biology

29 October 2021

Biology as Information Transmission

Language is information transmission, cellular communication is the language of the building blocks of life beginning from mini cellular beings, to multicellular organisms. The Biology zero week started with the introduction of information transmission of organisms, starting from quantum mechanics, chemistry, molecular biology, microbiology, cellular biology and physiology. During the very first morning session, I dove right into a new world of tiny mini-machines in my body and around me. Through the theory of human consciousness, gene modification and the ethics of genetic engineering, I learned that biology and genes, additionally to being a spelling/ language to sequence the building blocks of cells, also manifests questions of what makes , ethics and the democratization of information access and patents.

Notes on Synthetic biology and the anthropocene

Realms of Evolution: nature is the best designer

I learned that evolution is the machine for creating and that the constraints of growing melti-cellular beings make the parameters for evolution. Optimum design is the cycle of evolutions selective pressure. Evolution Is the genetic algorithm of generations of successful traits and iterative design.

Bio-Hacking Practical: Growing bacteria in medium

After learning about unicellular organisms, we prepared mediums to grow bacteria as a method to check and test microbes. We grew microbes after understanding the organisms they eat and metabolise. Other ways of classifying and studying organisms include information management ( eukaryote or prokaryote ), by kingdom, the colony shape ( gram positive or gram negative), sequencing and comparing bacteria communities.

Biological Terms I learnt

  • Phenotype- The physical properties and morphology of an organism. The genotype is the complete set of genes of an organism, also referring to its alleles and variant forms.
  • Metaheuristic - high-level procedure to find and generate a sufficiently good solution to an optimization problemVoronoi pattern: every point within a given region is closer to the “seed” inside that region than it is to any other point outside that region.
  • Metabolism- All biochemical processes and reactions in two ways: anabolism which is a bond-making process that requires ATP and catabolism, the bond breaking process.
  • Enzyme- A substance that acts as a catalyst in living organisms, regulating the rate at which chemical reactions proceed without itself being altered in the process. The biological processes that occur within all living organisms are chemical reactions, and most are regulated by enzymes.
  • Amino acids are organic compounds that contain amino and carboxyl functional groups, along with a side chain specific to each amino acid.

Agri-forestry and alternative farming

In the second week, we were introduced to new agroforestry methods, bee keeping and polycultures. We gained skills in soil analysing and understanding that the future of growing food could be computational. During the afternoons of the seminar, the MDEF class room transformed into a lab.

The practicals included:
  • Growing microorganism bacteria in different mediums we prepared in class.
  • Endosymbiotic Bacteria: Preparing solutions to grow spirulina and kombucha SCOBY.
  • Soil sampling testing.
  • Distilling and making essential oils.
